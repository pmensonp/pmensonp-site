My Story

Menson Poquie

Pronounced Poh-qwee.

I'm a Philadelphia native, born in Ivory Coast, from a family that's originally from Liberia. We left when I was two, after civil war broke out, and rebuilt in Philly.

Lacrosse and football raised me. At Haverford School I was a four-year letterwinner in both — helped lead our lacrosse team to a 26-0 season and the 2015 national championship, was named MVP of the Inter-Ac League title game, and finished as a top national recruit. On the football side I ran for over 1,000 yards and 13 touchdowns my senior year.

From there I played Division I lacrosse at Virginia. A couple years later I found a new home at West Chester, where I turned into one of the most dangerous return men in program history — I still rank top-five all-time at WCU in career kickoff return yards, kickoff returns, and punt return average.

Somewhere in there I picked up the itch to build things too — I'm working toward my MBA and studying tech, because the goal isn't just to go pro. It's to go pro and build something that outlasts the playing career.

Right now the target is simple: earn a pro tryout, and get to the 2028 Games.
